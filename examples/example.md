# Static Equilibrium

$$\sum F = 0$$

$$\sum M = 0$$

::: notes
:: Reference: Chapter 2, pages 45-52
:: Author: Pause between the two conditions for emphasis

[PRE 1s] [POST 2s]

The foundation of structural analysis is static equilibrium.
[PAUSE 2s]
For any structure to be stable, two conditions must be satisfied: the sum of all forces must equal zero, and the sum of all moments must equal zero.
[PAUSE 1.5s]
These are represented by the equations you see on the screen.
:::

# Beam Loading

Types of loads:

>- Point loads
>- Distributed loads  
>- Moment loads

::: notes
:: Author: Show examples on the whiteboard if time permits

[MIN 12s]

When analyzing beams, we need to consider different types of loading. First, point loads are concentrated forces applied at specific locations, like a person standing on a beam.

Second, distributed loads spread across a length of the beam, such as the weight of a roof.

Finally, moment loads cause rotation rather than translation, like a wrench turning a bolt.
:::

# Solution Approach

>- Draw free body diagram
>- Apply equilibrium equations
>- Solve for reactions
>- Check your work

::: notes
:: Author: Work through this step-by-step on the board

Here's how we'll solve this problem. Step one: Draw a free body diagram showing all forces and support reactions.

Step two: Apply our equilibrium equations, sum of forces equals zero and sum of moments equals zero.

Step three: Solve the resulting equations to find the reaction forces.

Step four: Always check your work by verifying that equilibrium is satisfied.
:::
